Example dataset

Let us assume that we are interested in students GPA. We believe that there are at least two variables that predict ones GPA (i.e., parental education and average time spent on homework per week. Datafile (L2.txt) contains three variables of interest, grades, pared, and hwork.

Variables 1) Grades (DV, Y): 8th grade students overall GPA in all subjects (100 point scale); 2) Parents education (IV1, X1): The level of Education of the students parents, in years of schooling (i.e., a high school graduate would have a score of 12, a college graduate a score of 16); 3) Average time spent on homework per week, in hours, across all subjects (IV2, X2).

WRITE-UP

The overall model is statistically significant (F(2,97) = 8.7, p = .0003), suggesting that at least one predictor is significant. The R-squared value of .15 suggests that 15% of variance in gpa was explained by the model. The unstandardized regression coefficient, b1, for Parent Education was 0.87, t(97) = 2.27, p = .026. For each additional year of parents schooling, expected student grades increased by 0.87 points, after controlling for the effect of time spent on homework. Of more direct interest was the coefficient associated with time spent on Homework, b2 = 0.99, t(97) = 2.74, p = .007. For each hour students spend on Homework per week, expected student grades increased by 0.99 points, after controlling for the effect of parent education.
